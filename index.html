About Me

Community, Connection, and Communication are the core of all of my endeavours. Science becomes meaningful when it is shared, questioned, and experienced together. My work focuses on building spaces where people can encounter science with curiosity rather than intimidation. Through workshops, public events, and creative outreach, I collaborate with communities, educators, and institutions to make science and arts outreach and education accessible, relational, and rooted in place.

Teaching & Learning Design

Teaching remains the foundation of my science communication practice. From 2013 to 2019, I taught introductory biology courses as a Primary Instructor and Teaching Assistant at Iowa State University. Teaching in a new country significantly shaped how I communicate science clearly, empathetically, and without assumptions about prior knowledge. In many ways, my students taught me a lot and for that I will always be grateful. I continued teaching after moving to Canada, through invited guest lectures in bioinformatics, biology, and parasitology.

I received the Teaching Excellence Award in 2015 for inclusive pedagogy and effective explanation of complex biological concepts.

Creative Science Alliance

I founded the Creative Science Alliance (CSA) to design hands-on, Calgary-based STEAM outreach rooted in local ecology and community contexts. My goal is to create a community of local scientists, artists, communicators, and change-makers to make science and arts education and outreach accessible and engaging in Calgary.

Arts and Science are two sides of the same coin and neither can be learnt or experienced in isolation. Through CSA, I want to help Calgarians connect with the city by focusing not only on observing and learning about our environment, but also on how this learning feels. We may not remember what we learned in second grade, but we most likely remember how that experience made us feel - and these feelings are a significant reason that made us who we are. At CSA, we encourage participants to focus equally on learning and feeling.

Editorial & Newsletter Leadership

I am passionate about designing newsletters and websites. As the Departmental Newsletter Coordinator at Iowa State University, I maintained the "2-1-1" newsletter to connect lecture and lab sections and improve the overall student experience.

Additionally, I founded and maintained a monthly newsletter called "Diversity Digest", which shared stories of biodiversity to increase student engagement. Students regularly contributed articles to the newsletter. While I started this newsletter for my sections, this newsletter was later shared across all sections in the course.

Public Engagement & Outreach

I have designed and facilitated hands-on science outreach activities across Calgary, both independently and through the Creative Science Alliance.

As the Head of Communications of the Graduate College at the University of Calgary, I oversaw team efforts to design and maintain the organization newsletter, website, blog, and social media.

As a member of the Host-Parasite Interactions Group at the University of Calgary, I led team efforts to organize multiple outreach events in Calgary. Examples include Fishtival and the Alberta Biodiversity Festival at the Bow Habitat Station and the Minds in Motion Summer Camps.
