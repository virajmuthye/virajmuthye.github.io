Teaching & Learning Design

Teaching and communication are inseparable. From 2013 to 2019, I taught biology as both a Primary Instructor and Teaching Assistant during my PhD at Iowa State University in the USA. Teaching in a new country shaped the way I communicate science clearly, empathetically, and without assumptions about prior knowledge.

I have continually sought opportunities to teach and continued doing so in Canada, serving as a guest lecturer for biology, parasitology, and bioinformatics courses at the University of Calgary.

I received the Teaching Excellence Award in 2015 for inclusive pedagogy and effective explanation of complex biological concepts. Teaching remains the foundation of my science communication practice.

Creative Science Alliance

I founded the Creative Science Alliance (CSA) to design hands-on, hyper-local STEAM outreach rooted in local ecology and community contexts in Calgary.

CSA functions as an applied science communication laboratory where I develop, test, and refine engagement strategies through STEAM camps, biodiversity activities, zines, and public programs for children and families.

Editorial & Newsletter Leadership

I have served as a departmental Newsletter Coordinator at Iowa State University, conceptualising, editing, and designing recurring science newsletters that integrated biology, equity, and community-building. I founded and maintained Diversity Digest, which highlighted fascinating examples of biodiversity to enhance engagement with course content in lectures and labs. Students also contributed to this newsletter. Additionally, I managed 2-1-1, connecting laboratory and lecture sections of Biology 211 to improve students’ overall learning experience.

This editorial experience informs my current public newsletter writing practice.

Public Engagement & Outreach

I design and lead hands-on science engagement initiatives that spark curiosity and inclusivity. My outreach work spans Canada and the USA, including biodiversity festivals, library programs, Ask-A-Scientist events, and community STEAM initiatives for children and families.

Leadership roles enhancing outreach include Executive-at-Large in the Graduate College at the University of Calgary (2023–2024), President of Sankalp, a non-profit at Iowa State University (2016–2017), Founder and President of Marathi Mandal, a student-run organization at Iowa State University (2018–2019), and website developer for Sankalp (2015–2017).
